# Heifer Zephyr — Voice & Tone

The brand voice for every Heifer Zephyr instrument document. This is what distinguishes a Heifer Zephyr brochure from a generic catalog entry: not the typeface, the prose.

## Source of truth

The canonical voice sample is the maker bio in the `tonykoop` profile README:

> *"Outside professional engineering, I build wooden instruments — drums and flutes I've been making and studying for 16+ years, beginning with drum building at Morgan Drums in St. Paul. The two practices inform each other; the same engineering instincts that make a stave-built djembe sound right are what make a medical device prototype assemble cleanly."*

That sentence does five things at once. It states what, with whom, where, for how long, and why. No hype. No adjectives. Engineering-honest. That is the voice.

## Voice rules

### 1. Engineering-honest, not marketing-puffy
- Say what the instrument is and how it works. Don't say it's "magical," "soulful," or "transformative."
- Numbers and physics are encouraged. *"Six paired-slit tongues, fundamental f₁ ≈ 261 Hz, body 1/4″ red oak"* beats *"a beautifully tuned drum that sings."*
- If the instrument has a flaw, name it before the buyer notices. Honesty is part of the product.

### 2. First-person when Tony is the subject; third-person when the instrument is the subject
- *"I built this drum to the WOOD magazine plan, then varied the wood species across three iterations."*
- *"The body is a paired-slit box, six tongues cut from a single 1/4″ top panel."*
- Not: *"Crafted by hand in Tony's California workshop."* (Self-marketing voice.)

### 3. Concrete over abstract
- Name the species, the dimension, the supplier, the date.
- *"Padauk top, Baltic-birch sides, finished in pure tung oil"* beats *"premium hardwoods, hand-finished."*

### 4. Provenance is the product
- Every instrument has a build location, a date range, a serial. Say them.
- Cross-reference: *"Sister study: tongue-drum repo, Phase 1 of the key-tuning DoE."*
- Cite reference plans honestly: *"Built to the WOOD magazine Oct 2008 plan (project: Steve Roberts; written: Owen Duvall)."*

### 5. Cross-discipline is fair game
- Tony's day job (medical-device R&D, additive manufacturing, tensile testing) informs the work and is allowed to surface.
- *"Tongue tuning was validated against the same f₁ ≈ 0.162 · (h/L²) · √(E/ρ) cantilever model used in the tongue-drum DoE study, with strike-level data collected on a 43-column schema borrowed from the medical-device tensile testing protocol."*

### 6. Voice changes by document type — but never breaks
- **Brochure:** warm, second-person-friendly, but every claim is testable.
- **Build method (workshop):** imperative, terse, safety-aware. *"Set the spoilboard. Verify zero. Drop the bit."*
- **Lab notebook:** declarative column headers + procedure steps, no prose.
- **Capstone / DoE report:** academic-honest. Hypotheses, methods, results, limitations.
- **Press kit:** maker bio + provenance. Quote-friendly.

## Words to use

build, made, cut, turned, tuned, validated, planned, sourced, finished, voiced, characterized, measured, instrumented, played, struck, blown, bent, bowed, plucked.

## Words to avoid

- **Marketing puffery:** "premium," "luxury," "artisanal," "soulful," "magical," "transformative," "elevated," "curated," "bespoke."
- **Generic craft tropes:** "handcrafted with love," "passion for music," "where art meets science."
- **AI-y filler:** "leveraging," "utilizing," "robust," "seamless," "innovative."
- **Hyperbole:** "perfect," "best-in-class," "unparalleled."

## Sentence-level patterns

### Opening a brochure
> *"This is a small wooden tongue drum built to the WOOD magazine Oct 2008 plan, with three deliberate departures from the original."*

### Closing a sales page
> *"It's tuned, it's signed, and it ships in a fitted box."*

### Spec-line for a hangtag
> *"TNG-001 · Heifer Zephyr · Red oak top, walnut body · D minor / A minor · Built 2026, Santa Clara, CA"*

### Press-kit bio one-liner
> *"Tony Koop is a mechanical R&D engineer who builds wooden drums and flutes. The instruments and the engineering inform each other."*
